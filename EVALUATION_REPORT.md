# Evaluation Report: HR Policy Q&A Fine-Tuning

## 1. Executive Summary

This report documents the evaluation of a two-stage fine-tuning pipeline (LoRA SFT followed by DPO) applied to Mistral 7B Instruct v0.2 for HR policy question answering. The system was trained on preference data generated by a RAG pipeline (GPT-4o + ChromaDB) over 533 HR policy documents and employee handbooks.

**Key results:**
- **SFT** achieved a **1.78x improvement in ROUGE-L** (0.164 → 0.293), demonstrating successful domain adaptation with concise, policy-grounded responses
- **DPO** further improved preference quality — preferred by GPT-4o-mini judge over SFT in **62.6%** and over Base in **57.6%** of comparisons
- The full RLHF pipeline (SFT + DPO) works end-to-end within Kaggle's free GPU tier

## 2. Experimental Setup

## 4. LLM-as-Judge Evaluation

### 4.1 Setup

- **Judge model:** GPT-4o-mini
- **Method:** Pairwise comparison — for each prompt, the judge receives both responses and picks a winner based on helpfulness, accuracy, relevance, and conciseness
- **Comparisons:** 3 pairwise matchups × 198 prompts = 594 total judgments
- **Errors:** 0 (all judgments valid)

### 4.2 Results

| Comparison | Model A Wins | Model B Wins | Winner |
|------------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Base vs SFT | Base: 144 (72.7%) | SFT: 54 (27.3%) | **Base** |
| SFT vs DPO | SFT: 74 (37.4%) | DPO: 124 (62.6%) | **DPO** |
| Base vs DPO | Base: 84 (42.4%) | DPO: 114 (57.6%) | **DPO** |

### 4.3 Analysis: Two Metrics, Two Stories

The ROUGE-L and LLM judge metrics tell complementary stories:

**ROUGE-L says SFT is best** — it learned to produce concise, policy-specific answers that closely match the reference text. This is a measure of factual alignment.

**The judge says DPO is best** — it prefers DPO's more detailed, well-structured answers over SFT's shorter ones. This is a measure of perceived quality.

**Why does the judge prefer Base over SFT?** Verbosity bias. The judge lacks access to actual HR policy documents and cannot verify factual accuracy. Base writes 202 words of confident-sounding generic advice (citing FLSA, ADA, general best practices). SFT writes 131 words of correct but concise policy-specific answers. Without ground truth, the judge rewards thoroughness.

**Why does the judge prefer DPO?** DPO learned the balance — it writes more substantive answers than SFT (178 vs 131 words) while being more focused than Base (178 vs 202 words). It also has better structure and no hallucination instances.

**Key insight:** DPO optimized for what humans (or an LLM proxy) prefer, not for text overlap. This is exactly its design intent.

### 4.4 Illustrative Example

**Prompt:** "What is the company match percentage for the retirement plan?"

| Model | ROUGE-L | Response Summary |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Base (0.146) | Generic | "I'll need to clarify... this percentage can vary greatly from one company to another..." |
| SFT (0.231) | Concise | "I'd need to refer to the specific HR policy documents... Please let me know if you have access" |
| DPO (0.411) | Best | "Without specific context, I cannot provide an answer. Please refer to your organization's HR policy documents or contact the HR department directly" |

DPO gives the most appropriate response — acknowledging the limitation while directing to the right resource.

## 5. Qualitative Analysis

### 5.1 What SFT Learned Well

- **Policy-specific details:** Correctly references compensatory off rules, remote work policies, company-specific processes
- **Appropriate hedging:** When information isn't in training data, redirects to HR/policy documents
- **Concise formatting:** Learned bullet points and structured answers matching reference style
- **Highest ROUGE-L:** Closest to the ground-truth policy answers

### 5.2 What DPO Added

- **Better structure:** More organized, numbered responses with clear formatting
- **Preference alignment:** Produces responses that external evaluators prefer
- **Zero hallucination instances:** No cases of verbose off-topic responses
- **Balanced verbosity:** More detailed than SFT without Base's generic padding

### 5.3 Base Model Behavior

- Provides **generic, textbook-style** answers citing US federal law (FLSA, ADA)
- Uses **hedging language** ("I'd be happy to help, but...")
- Generates **longest responses** (202 words avg) with general best practices
- Never references any specific company policy or internal process

## 9. Conclusion

The full RLHF pipeline successfully adapted Mistral 7B to the HR policy domain:

1. **SFT** taught the model to produce concise, policy-grounded answers (ROUGE-L: 0.164 → 0.293, +78% improvement), with responses 35% shorter than Base and closer to reference style.

2. **DPO** refined the model for human preferences — preferred by an LLM judge over SFT (62.6%) and over Base (57.6%), with zero hallucination instances and better response structure.

3. The two evaluation methods (ROUGE-L and LLM judge) capture complementary quality dimensions: factual alignment vs perceived quality. Both confirm that fine-tuning improved the model, with each stage contributing differently.

4. The entire pipeline — RAG, preference collection, SFT, DPO, evaluation — runs within Kaggle's free GPU tier (~2.5 hours total), demonstrating that meaningful RLHF can be done with minimal compute resources.
